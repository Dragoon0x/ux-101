# Microcopy Craft
**Purpose:** Write the tiny text that makes interfaces usable — button labels, placeholder text, helper text, confirmation messages, and the micro-moments that determine whether users feel confident or confused.

Microcopy is the most impactful writing in any product. A single confusing button label can cause 20% drop-off. A single clear helper text can reduce support tickets by 50%. The small words carry big weight because they appear at decision moments — the exact points where users are deciding what to do.

## Button Copy
Describe the outcome, not the action. "Create account" not "Submit." "Send invitation" not "Done." "Start free trial" not "Continue." Front-load the verb: "Save draft" not "Click to save draft." Use sentence case: "Get started" not "GET STARTED" (all-caps reduces reading speed by 13-20%).

## Placeholder Text
Placeholders should show format, not repeat the label. Label: "Phone number." Placeholder: "(555) 123-4567" — not "Enter your phone number." Placeholders disappear on focus, so they must not contain information the user needs while typing. Never use placeholder as the only label — it fails accessibility and disappears when users need it most.

## Helper Text
One sentence below the input. Anticipate the question: "We'll use this to send your receipt" under the email field. Prevent the error: "Password must be 8+ characters with one number" under the password field. Helper text is preventive medicine — it stops errors before they happen, which is faster than fixing them after.
