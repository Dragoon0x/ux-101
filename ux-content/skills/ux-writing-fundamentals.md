# Ux Writing Fundamentals
**Purpose:** Write interface text that helps users accomplish their goals — clear, concise, useful, and human-sounding without being cute.

UX writing is functional writing. Every word in an interface has a job — to help the user understand where they are, what they can do, and what will happen when they do it. Words that don't do one of these jobs are words that slow the user down.

## The Three Principles
Clear: use plain language, not jargon. "Upload a file" not "Ingest an artifact." Concise: use as few words as possible. "Save" not "Click here to save your changes." Useful: tell users what they need to know, not what you want to say. "Your password must be 8+ characters" not "We take security seriously."

## The Voice Consistency
Interface voice should be consistent — the same product shouldn't sound corporate in error messages and casual in tooltips. Define the voice as 3-4 adjectives (e.g., "Clear, confident, friendly, not clever") and apply them to every text element. The voice should sound like one person wrote the entire product.

## The Word Count Standard
Buttons: 1-3 words. Labels: 1-4 words. Helper text: 1 sentence. Error messages: 1-2 sentences. Tooltips: 1-2 sentences. Empty states: 1-3 sentences. Page titles: 2-5 words. If the text is longer than these limits, it's probably trying to do too much in one element.
