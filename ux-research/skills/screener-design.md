# Screener Design
**Purpose:** Design screening questionnaires that filter participants accurately — because testing with the wrong users produces insights that are worse than no insights.

A screener has one job: separate people who match your target user from people who don't, without revealing what the 'right' answers are. The moment a screener telegraphs what you're looking for, you get professional research participants gaming their way in.

## The Structure
Start with demographic qualifiers (age, location, role) to eliminate obvious mismatches. Then behavioral qualifiers — what tools they use, how often, for what purpose. Embed the target behavior among plausible alternatives so participants can't guess which answer gets them selected. End with logistical questions (availability, technology setup for remote research).

## The Critical Rules
Never ask leading questions ("Do you find X frustrating?" — everyone says yes). Use multiple-choice with a correct answer hidden among plausible options. Include a 'none of the above' option to catch guessers. Disqualify anyone who selects contradictory answers. Aim for a 20-30% qualification rate — if everyone qualifies, the screener isn't filtering.

## The Sample
For usability testing: 5-8 participants per user segment. For interviews: 8-12 until you reach saturation (hearing the same themes repeated). For surveys: minimum 100 for directional data, 400+ for statistical significance with subgroup analysis.
