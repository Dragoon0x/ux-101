# Interview Guide Design
**Purpose:** Design interview guides that produce deep, honest, actionable insights — structured enough to be comparable across participants, flexible enough to follow unexpected threads.

The interview guide is not a script. It's a roadmap with planned destinations and permission to take detours. The structure ensures you cover the essential topics with every participant. The flexibility ensures you can pursue the unexpected revelations that make qualitative research valuable.

## The Guide Structure
5 sections, 45-60 minutes total. Opening (5 min): context questions that warm up the participant and establish their relationship with the problem space. Core exploration (20 min): the critical incident questions — "walk me through the last time you..." Deeper probing (10 min): follow the most interesting thread from the core section. Specific topics (10 min): questions about particular features, concepts, or designs you need input on. Closing (5 min): "anything else I should have asked?" — this question regularly produces the best insight of the entire interview.

## The Question Types
Use open-ended questions (tell me about...), not closed questions (do you like...). Use past-tense questions (what did you do last time) not hypothetical (what would you do). Use follow-up probes: "tell me more about that," "what happened next," "why did you choose that approach," "how did that make you feel."

## The Anti-Patterns
Never ask "would you use this?" (everyone says yes). Never ask "how much would you pay?" (nobody knows). Never explain the product before asking about the problem. Never fill silence — count to 5 after every answer before asking the next question.
