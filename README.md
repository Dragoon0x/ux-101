## The Philosophy

UX is not a phase. It's not a department. It's the quality of the experience that determines whether a product succeeds or fails.

The gap between knowing UX principles and applying them is enormous. Most UX knowledge lives in scattered blog posts, expensive courses, and tribal knowledge that never gets documented. The result is inconsistency — every designer reinventing frameworks, every team debating first principles, every product making the same mistakes that have been solved a thousand times.

**That's what this repo is.** Every proven UX method, pattern, framework, and principle — encoded as skills that AI agents can apply and humans can reference. Not theory. Practice.

The hierarchy is:
1. **Research** — Do you actually understand the people you're designing for?
2. **Strategy** — Are your design decisions connected to a coherent plan?
3. **Information Architecture** — Can people find what they need?
4. **Interaction Design** — Does the product feel right to use?
5. **Visual Design** — Does the visual layer communicate clearly?
6. **Accessibility** — Does it work for everyone?
7. **Content** — Do the words help or hinder?
8. **Prototyping** — Can you test before you build?
9. **Platform** — Does it respect the conventions users expect?
10. **Specialized Domains** — Does it work for the specific industry context?

Get the hierarchy right and you build products people love. Skip a layer and you build products people tolerate — until they find something better.

## The Domains

### 1. ux-research — *98 skills, 6 commands*
*User research methods, discovery, synthesis, and the discipline of understanding people before designing for them.*

Research is the foundation. Every design decision not grounded in user understanding is a guess — and guesses at scale are expensive.

**Commands:** `/research-plan` · `/interview` · `/survey` · `/usability-test` · `/synthesis` · `/research-ops`

---

### 2. ux-strategy — *94 skills, 6 commands*
*Design strategy, product-design alignment, UX vision, and the thinking that makes design decisions compound.*

Strategy connects individual design decisions into a coherent system. Without it, design is a collection of well-crafted screens that don't add up to a product.

**Commands:** `/ux-strategy` · `/design-vision` · `/ux-audit` · `/experience-principles` · `/design-roadmap` · `/ux-pitch`

---

### 3. ux-ia — *87 skills, 6 commands*
*Content organization, navigation design, labeling, search, and the invisible structure that determines whether people can find anything.*

If users can't find it, it doesn't exist. Information architecture is the invisible structure that makes complex systems navigable.

**Commands:** `/ia` · `/navigation` · `/taxonomy` · `/search` · `/sitemap` · `/wayfinding`

---

### 4. ux-interaction — *103 skills, 6 commands*
*Interaction patterns, input design, feedback systems, gestures, and the moment-to-moment experience of using a product.*

Interaction design is what the product feels like to use — not how it looks, but how it responds, communicates, and handles every moment.

**Commands:** `/interaction` · `/flow` · `/feedback` · `/form` · `/gesture` · `/microinteraction`

---

### 5. ux-visual — *90 skills, 6 commands*
*Visual design for interfaces — typography, color, spacing, iconography, and the visual language that makes products clear.*

Visual design is not about making things pretty. It's about making things clear. The visual layer communicates hierarchy, state, relationships, and brand simultaneously.

**Commands:** `/visual-direction` · `/ui-audit` · `/icon-system` · `/color-system` · `/type-system` · `/visual-polish`

---

### 6. ux-accessibility — *112 skills, 6 commands*
*Designing for everyone — WCAG compliance, assistive technology, inclusive design, and the practices that make products truly universal.*

Accessibility is not an edge case. 15-20% of people have some form of disability. Designing for accessibility makes products better for everyone.

**Commands:** `/a11y-audit` · `/a11y-fix` · `/a11y-system` · `/a11y-test` · `/screen-reader` · `/a11y-checklist`

---

### 7. ux-content — *104 skills, 6 commands*
*UX writing, content design, microcopy, voice & tone, and the words that make interfaces usable and human.*

Words are the interface. Every label, message, button, and instruction is a design decision. UX writing is the craft of making interfaces speak clearly.

**Commands:** `/microcopy` · `/voice-tone` · `/error-copy` · `/onboarding-copy` · `/content-audit` · `/content-system`

---

### 8. ux-prototyping — *97 skills, 6 commands*
*Prototyping methods, fidelity decisions, testing with prototypes, and the fastest path from idea to validated design.*

Prototyping is thinking with your hands. The fastest way to learn whether a design works is to build it just enough to test it.

**Commands:** `/prototype` · `/wireframe` · `/test-plan` · `/fidelity` · `/rapid-prototype` · `/prototype-review`

---

### 9. ux-platform — *102 skills, 6 commands*
*Platform-specific design for web, iOS, Android, desktop, wearable, TV, AR/VR, and cross-platform consistency.*

Every platform has conventions, constraints, and user expectations. Designing for a platform means respecting its patterns while maintaining product identity.

**Commands:** `/platform` · `/responsive` · `/cross-platform` · `/native-vs-web` · `/platform-audit` · `/adaptive`

---

### 10. ux-specialized — *114 skills, 6 commands*
*Domain-specific UX for e-commerce, SaaS, enterprise, healthcare, finance, gaming, AI products, and beyond.*

Different domains have different user expectations, regulatory requirements, and design challenges. Generic UX principles need domain-specific adaptation.

**Commands:** `/domain-ux` · `/saas-ux` · `/ecommerce-ux` · `/enterprise-ux` · `/ai-ux` · `/dashboard-ux`
