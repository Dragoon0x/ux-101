# Design Sprint Facilitation
**Purpose:** Facilitate a design sprint that produces a tested prototype in 5 days — the compressed process that replaces months of debate with a week of action.

The design sprint (Google Ventures framework) compresses problem understanding, solution ideation, decision-making, prototyping, and testing into one week. It works because the time constraint forces decisions that committees would deliberate for months, and the testing on Friday prevents the team from falling in love with untested ideas.

## The Daily Structure
Monday: Map the problem, choose a target. Tuesday: Sketch competing solutions individually (no groupthink). Wednesday: Decide which solution to prototype using structured voting (dot voting, then Decider breaks ties). Thursday: Build a realistic-looking prototype (not functional, just believable). Friday: Test with 5 real users and watch the patterns emerge.

## The Facilitator's Job
Keep the clock ruthless — every exercise is timeboxed. Protect the introverts — individual sketching before group discussion. Prevent the highest-paid person from dominating — use democratic voting methods. Make decisions final — no revisiting Monday's decisions on Wednesday.

## The Common Failures
Skipping Friday testing (the sprint becomes a design exercise, not a learning exercise). Inviting too many people (7 max). Not having a Decider with real authority. Prototyping too high-fidelity (it only needs to fool a user for 30 minutes).
