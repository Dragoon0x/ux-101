# UX Strategy Development
**Purpose:** Build a UX strategy that aligns design decisions with business goals and user needs — the framework that makes every design decision reinforce every other one.

UX without strategy is craft without direction. Beautiful screens that don't serve the business. Usable flows that don't differentiate the product. Consistent patterns that consistently deliver the wrong experience. Strategy is what connects design decisions to outcomes — ensuring that every pixel, every interaction, every word serves both the user and the business.

## The Three-Legged Stool
UX strategy sits at the intersection of three forces: user needs (what people want and struggle with), business goals (what the company needs to achieve), and technical feasibility (what can actually be built). A strategy that ignores any leg falls over. The best strategies find the sweet spot where all three align — where solving user problems also drives business metrics and is technically achievable.

## The Experience Differentiation
Most products in most categories offer similar functionality. The experience is the differentiation. UX strategy defines what the experience differentiation is — the specific quality of the experience that makes users choose this product over alternatives. Speed. Simplicity. Delight. Power. Trust. The strategy names it and every design decision reinforces it.

## The Priority Framework
You can't fix everything at once. UX strategy defines the priority: which user problems matter most, which experience gaps are most damaging, which improvements would have the highest impact. The priority framework prevents the common failure of spreading design resources across too many initiatives and improving nothing meaningfully.

## When to use
At product inception. When the product has grown without design direction. When design decisions feel arbitrary. When the team can't articulate why the UX is the way it is.
