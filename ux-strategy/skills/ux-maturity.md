# UX Maturity Assessment
**Purpose:** Assess where the organization is on the UX maturity spectrum and build a plan to advance — because the practices that work at maturity level 2 are different from those that work at level 5.

UX maturity is not about how good the design team is. It's about how deeply user experience thinking is embedded in the organization's decision-making. A mature UX organization doesn't just have good designers — it has product managers who think in user terms, engineers who advocate for usability, and executives who fund research.

## The Maturity Levels
Level 1: Absent — no dedicated UX practice. Level 2: Limited — UX exists but is purely tactical (making things look nice). Level 3: Emergent — UX influences some decisions through research and design. Level 4: Structured — UX is integrated into the product development process with clear roles and methods. Level 5: Integrated — UX thinking pervades the organization; user needs are a primary input to business strategy.

## The Advancement Strategy
You can't jump levels. Each level requires specific changes. Level 1→2 needs a dedicated design hire and basic tools. Level 2→3 needs research capability and executive awareness. Level 3→4 needs process integration and cross-functional collaboration. Level 4→5 needs cultural change and executive commitment.

## The Common Traps
Hiring a design leader at maturity level 2 and expecting level 5 practices. Investing in a design system before the team has the discipline to use it. Running research that nobody acts on because the organization doesn't have the process to integrate findings.

## When to use
When assessing the current state of UX in the organization. When building a UX team strategy. When advocating for UX investment. When a design leader joins a new organization.
